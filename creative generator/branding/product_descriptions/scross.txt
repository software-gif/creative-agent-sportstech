The sCross Crosstrainer is a sleek, modern, and robust fitness machine, predominantly finished in a matte black, slightly textured plastic and metal, giving it a sophisticated appearance. Its overall silhouette is characterized by a sturdy, rectangular base with rounded corners, from which a gracefully curved, thick upright structure rises, leaning slightly forward. This main body houses the central mechanism, covered on the sides by prominent, smooth, light gray/silver circular discs. The machine is taller than it is wide or deep when in use, reaching approximately chest to shoulder height for an average person, with the movable handlebars extending even higher. Extending forward from the central mechanism are two long, polished silver/chrome rails, along which large, black, textured plastic pedals with raised edges glide in an elliptical path. The machine features two sets of handlebars: two tall, curved, movable arm poles that extend from the lower-mid section of the main body, curving upwards and slightly inwards, topped with thick, soft black foam/rubber grips; and two shorter, fixed, horizontal grips located below the display console, also with black foam/rubber grips.

At the top of the main upright structure, angled slightly upwards and towards the user, is a rectangular display console with a black bezel. This console features a tablet holder at the very top, currently displaying a grid of video thumbnails from a fitness app. Below the tablet holder, a dedicated digital screen shows workout metrics with a blue background and white/light blue text and graphics. Directly beneath this screen, and above the fixed grips, is a prominent circular control knob, black with a silver/chrome ring, likely featuring a small central digital display for resistance or time. No other distinct buttons are visible on the console or handlebars, though heart rate sensors might be integrated into the fixed grips. A stylized "S" logo, in light gray/silver, is subtly placed on the upper-mid section of the main curved body. Black cables are neatly routed along the movable handlebars and the main frame. The machine is designed for an upright, standing user who faces the display. Feet are placed securely on the large pedals, which perform a smooth, joint-friendly elliptical gliding motion, never leaving the rails. Hands can either grasp the movable arm poles for a full-body push/pull workout, or the fixed grips for lower-body focus and stability. Small transport wheels are visible at the front of the base, and the entire front section with the pedal arms and rails can pivot upwards and backwards, allowing the machine to fold into a more compact, vertical position for storage.
