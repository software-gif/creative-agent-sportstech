The X150 Ergometer 3in1 is a compact, foldable exercise bike featuring a distinctive X-frame design, predominantly in matte black with vibrant red accents. Its overall silhouette is characterized by two main curved, tubular metal bars that intersect in the middle, forming the "X" shape, allowing it to be folded for storage or expanded for use. When in use, it stands roughly chest-to-shoulder height relative to a seated adult. The primary body color is a matte black finish on the metal frame and the plastic casing of the flywheel. Red serves as the main accent color, appearing as a prominent stripe on the side of the flywheel casing, on the padded seat cushion and the oval-shaped backrest cushion, as well as on the inner grips of the main handlebars, the ends of the recumbent handlebars, and the central part of the resistance adjustment knob. The display is a rectangular LCD screen with slightly rounded corners, positioned centrally on the handlebar post, just below the main handlebars. It has a black bezel with a subtle red accent strip along its bottom edge and is angled slightly upwards for optimal viewing, showing digital readouts like time, speed, distance, and calories. The main handlebars are a curved, U-shaped design, extending upwards and outwards from the display post, finished in matte black. Below these, flanking the display, are two shorter, straight horizontal grips with red inner sections, likely serving as pulse sensors. Additionally, two shorter, straight handlebars extend forward from the sides of the seat, also black with red accents on their ends, providing support for a recumbent riding position. Controls include a circular resistance knob, black with a red center, located on the front post below the display, and small, dark gray buttons (e.g., Mode, Set, Reset) integrated into the display's bezel. A silver-colored, spring-loaded pin or lever is visible on the X-frame, facilitating the folding mechanism, alongside a red lever for seat height adjustment. Branding includes "SPORTSTECH" in white, sans-serif text, often accompanied by a small logo, prominently displayed on the matte black flywheel casing. There are no visible LED strips or lights on the body itself, only the illuminated display screen. The machine is constructed from metal tubing for the frame, plastic for the flywheel casing and pedals, and padded fabric/faux leather for the seat and backrest, with rubber or foam grips on the handlebars. Structural details include visible bolts and connection points, thin black cables running from the display area down to the flywheel, and horizontal stabilizer bars at the front and rear with black plastic end caps. For human interaction, the user always faces forward, placing their feet on the black plastic pedals which feature adjustable straps. Hands can be placed on the main curved handlebars, the inner pulse grips, or the lower recumbent handlebars extending from the seat. Integrated power ropes, with black handles, extend from the front post near the display, allowing for upper body engagement while pedaling. The machine supports an upright posture, a relaxed recumbent posture leaning against the backrest, or a combined full-body workout using the power ropes. Incorrect usage would involve facing away from the display, feet not on pedals, or the machine appearing unstable or improperly assembled.
