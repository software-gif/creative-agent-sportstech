The WoodPad Pro is a sleek, compact, and low-profile walking pad characterized by its elegant design, primarily featuring a light, natural wood finish. Its overall form factor is a long, narrow, flat rectangular base with a distinctively raised and smoothly curved front section. The machine sits very close to the ground, with the black walking surface only a few inches high, making it ideal for placement under a standing desk. The main body, encompassing the side panels and the entire front curved section, is crafted from a light-colored wood, resembling light oak or birch, exhibiting a clear wood grain texture and a smooth, possibly satin finish. The walking belt itself is a matte black, textured, non-slip material. The internal frame surrounding the walking belt and the control panel area are also matte black. Small, dark transport wheels with metallic silver accents are visible underneath the front section, designed for easy maneuverability. Crucially, there are no handlebars or upright supports; the product is designed for free walking. The control panel is integrated into the matte black top surface of the raised front section. It features a small, rectangular digital display with slightly rounded corners, showing white/light numbers (e.g., "0.0" or "0.8") against a dark background. Directly below the display are three circular, black buttons with white symbols: a central play/pause button, and flanking minus and plus buttons for speed adjustment. The brand name "SPORTSTECH" is prominently displayed in white, sans-serif, uppercase text on the left side of the black walking belt, running parallel to its length, accompanied by a stylized 'S' logo. A black power cable port and a red power switch are located on the right side of the front section. The wooden side panels curve seamlessly into the front control panel, giving it a modern, integrated appearance. There are no visible LED accents. When in use, a person stands upright, facing forward towards the display, with their feet placed directly on the black walking belt, maintaining a natural walking posture. Hands are free, often engaged with a phone or a laptop on a standing desk, as there are no grips to hold.
